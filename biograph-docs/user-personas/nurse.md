# Nurse – Daily Workflow & Navigation Guide

A Nurse supports clinical care by recording patient vitals, executing doctor instructions, managing patient activities, and coordinating care across outpatient and inpatient settings.  
This page provides a structured overview of daily nursing workflows to help navigate Biograph efficiently.

## 🔹 How to Use This Page
Use this page as a quick navigation guide. Each workflow represents a common nursing task and shows the exact system path to follow.

## 🔹 Core Nursing Workflow (Primary Loop)
Support patient care from preparation to execution.

**Workflow**
Patient Appointment → Vital Signs → Patient Encounter → Service Request / Medication Request → Nursing Task → Progress Notes

**Steps Explained**
- **Patient Appointment** – Identify and manage arriving patients  
- **Vital Signs** – Record patient health indicators  
- **Patient Encounter** – Assist in consultation and add observations  
- **Service Request / Medication Request** – Execute doctor orders  
- **Nursing Task** – Track assigned care activities  
- **Progress Notes (Encounter / Inpatient Record)** – Document patient condition  

## 🔹 Daily Jobs To Be Done

### 1. Record Patient Vitals
Capture key health indicators before or during consultation.  
**Workflow**  
Patient Appointment → Vital Signs → Link to Patient Encounter  

### 2. Manage Patient Check-In & Queue
Assist in patient flow and preparation before consultation.  
**Workflow**  
Patient Appointment → Check-In → Queue Management  

### 3. Prepare Patient for Consultation
Collect initial information and prepare patient for doctor.  
**Workflow**  
Patient Appointment → Vital Signs → Patient Encounter (Pre-consultation notes)  

### 4. Execute Nursing Tasks
Perform tasks assigned by doctors and track completion.  
**Workflow**  
Patient Encounter → Service Request / Medication Request → Nursing Task  

### 5. Administer Medications (OPD/IPD)
Provide medications as prescribed and record administration.  
**Workflow**  
Medication Request → Administer Medication → Update Status  

### 6. Administer Inpatient Medications
Manage medication orders for admitted patients.  
**Workflow**  
Inpatient Record → Inpatient Medication Order → Inpatient Medication Entry  

### 7. Create & Manage Service Requests
Coordinate lab tests, procedures, and therapy tasks.  
**Workflow**  
Patient Encounter → Service Request → Track Status  

### 8. Manage Inpatient Care (IPD)
Provide continuous care for admitted patients.  
**Workflow**  
Inpatient Record → Vital Signs → Medication Orders → Nursing Task → Progress Notes → Discharge Planning  

### 9. Monitor Patient Condition & Symptoms
Track patient health changes and escalate when needed.  
**Workflow**  
Patient Encounter / Inpatient Record → Vital Signs → Observation → Progress Notes  

### 10. Document Patient Observations
Maintain accurate nursing notes and patient updates.  
**Workflow**  
Patient Encounter / Inpatient Record → Progress Notes  

## 🔹 Key Navigation Insight

Most nursing activities revolve around three core areas:

- **Patient Appointment** – Entry point for patient flow and preparation  
- **Patient Encounter** – Central point for clinical coordination and task execution  
- **Inpatient Record** – Continuous care management for admitted patients  

From these, nurses can:
- record vital signs  
- execute doctor orders  
- manage nursing tasks and service requests  
- administer medications  
- monitor and document patient condition  

Understanding these core modules enables efficient navigation of daily nursing workflows.
